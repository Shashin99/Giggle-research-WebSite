Literature Survey

Enhancing concentration and practical thinking of students with dyscalculia

Function 01 : Adaptive Question Generating Math Skill Assessment

General cognition training websites such as Lumosity [1] and CogniFit [2] improve attention and memory but lack domain-specific features for dyscalculia relative to software such as “Nanashilpa” [3], “EdSense” [4] and “Sankalpa Math Master” [5]. [6] describes a screening and intervention framework using Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN), Support Vector Machines (SVM) and Random Forest to screen for dyscalculia and dysgraphia in Sri Lankan primary schools, but CNNs require large datasets while SVMs are challenged to address variation in behavior. The existing assessment systems still lack adaptive questioning and individualized intervention. Future studies must integrate AI-based personalized learning approaches to enhance dyscalculia diagnosis and intervention, ensuring tailored support for disabled students.

Function 02 : Interventions to Address Semantic Dyscalculia

Research on German dyscalculic students [7] shows that the integration of sensual stimuli, mixed reality simulators, and virtual theaters in Virtual Learning Environments (VLE) based on "Dybuster Calcularis" is effective. However, it lacks real-time behavior monitoring to ascertain student engagement and emotional responses. Study [8], an electronic resource in Kuala Lumpur, Mont Kiara, improves concentration and interest using pictorial content and pedagogies but does not have interactive exercises of visualization through which students may explore concepts in a dynamic sense. “D-KNIGHTS” a fourth-grade dyscalculic students' 3D game, promotes strategic mathematical thinking through drag-and-drop exercises and teacher-controlled learning [9]. Comparatively, the gamification system [10] applies a Game Development Life Cycle (GDLC) approach with three gaming methods: Piano Game, Drawer Game, and Harvest Fruit Game, based on visual contrast. Despite there exist loopholes in monitoring students' emotions, embedding interactive visualization, and adaptive practice drills.

Function 03: Interventions to Address Procedural Dyscalculia

Students should be taught to understand and follow mathematical procedures, rules and sequences. "Kidkanit" explores how to assist dyscalculic students in mastering the fundamentals of mathematics through computer-assisted instruction (CAI). However, it lacks a systematic approach to teaching and practicing procedural mathematics, which is essential for longterm retention [11]. The article "Hand Gesture Recognition with Inertial Sensors" highlights the potential of an inertial sensor-based approach to hand gesture recognition to enhance dyscalculic students' math learning and problem-solving ability [12]. Students with graphical dyscalculia are guided in writing numbers through lessons by "Ganitha Piyasa". Using the MNIST dataset and an Artificial Neural Network (ANN), their performance is assessed in terms of accuracy and completion time, with 98.3% accuracy achievement but does not examine students' procedural understanding [13]. Similarly, the system [14] evaluated CNN-based handwriting recognition, proving its effectiveness in detecting numerical writing errors, using the MNIST dataset for test benchmark due to its large population and variability. Despite advancements, gaps are still present in procedural math practice, gesture monitoring, handwriting analysis, and monitoring learning progress for dyscalculic students.

Function 04 : Interventions to Address Verbal Dyscalculia

Research [15]identifies verbal dyscalculia in primary school children, where users are prompted to select correct responses based on background voice instructions. It employs a CNN model with 91% accuracy and a 0.32 test error, which helps in early detection, however, target do not detect pronunciation issues. Study [16] introduced a voice chat feature via Natural Language Processing (NLP) and voice recognition to assist dyscalculic pupils in providing responses verbally but it lacks proper structuring in how oral math exercises are instructed, as only elementary interaction is accommodated. Study [17] proves that deep learning algorithms enhance mathematics understanding by way of chatbot-supported verbal communication, helping kids with pronunciation. Nevertheless, it does not offer real-time assessment of verbal skill, restricting it to monitor improvements in the long run. A clever voice conversation system [18] for Android augments speech recognition and synthesis, highlighting the role of saying numbers out loud in mathematical education. Nevertheless, it does not integrate adaptive learning with respect to verbal answers.

Research Gaps

The following research gaps have been identified based on recent studies and highlight the limitations of current educational and technological interventions for dyscalculia.

Lack of Interactive and Visual Tools for Semantic Dyscalculia

Current platforms prioritize speed and accuracy but often overlook meaningful number comprehension. The absence of interactive visuals and emotional engagement limits support for students with semantic dyscalculia. Augmented Reality (AR) can bridge this gap by enabling intuitive, sensory-driven exploration of mathematical concepts.

Absence of Continuous and Adaptive Learning Support

Most existing systems focus solely on initial dyscalculia detection using models like SVM and Random Forest. They fail to provide ongoing progress tracking or adaptive interventions, limiting their capacity to meet evolving student needs over time.

Inadequate Step-by-Step Support for Procedural Dyscalculia

Students with procedural dyscalculia benefit from structured, step-by-step problem-solving guidance. However, current tools lack interactive scaffolding and real-time feedback mechanisms, making it difficult for learners to build consistent procedural understanding and confidence.

Limited Support for Verbal Dyscalculia and Speech-Based Learning

Verbal dyscalculia, which affects the ability to understand or articulate mathematical language, is underrepresented in current systems. Few platforms offer structured verbal exercises with real-time speech recognition, pronunciation feedback, or adaptive oral learning tools that could enhance verbal numeracy.

Research Problem & Solution

From Problem to Solution: Advancing Children's Learning Environment while enhancing practical thinking

Problem

How can a mobile-based adaptive learning platform be developed to provide continuous, individualized support for students with dyscalculia particularly addressing semantic, procedural, and verbal subtypes by integrating technologies such as Augmented Reality, step-by-step interactive guidance, and real-time voice and emotion monitoring, to improve their conceptual understanding, verbal numeracy, and mathematical confidence beyond initial diagnosis?


Proposed Solution

To address the gaps in dyscalculia intervention, the proposed solution is a mobile-based adaptive learning platform that offers continuous support tailored to each student's needs. It uses an adaptive learning engine that adjusts question difficulty in real time, along with Augmented Reality (AR) features to help students with semantic dyscalculia visualize and interact with math concepts. Emotional and behavioral monitoring is also included to detect frustration and adjust the learning experience accordingly


For procedural dyscalculia, the system provides step-by-step guided problem-solving with instant feedback, helping students follow structured methods and build confidence. To support verbal dyscalculia, it includes voice recognition and NLP tools that allow students to practice verbal math skills and receive feedback on pronunciation. Together, these tools create a personalized, engaging, and supportive learning environment.

Assessing how well the student knows basic mathematics and provides a personalized course content

This goal aims to assess each student’s math skills using an adaptive test that adjusts question difficulty in real time. By analyzing response accuracy, speed, and patterns, the system creates a learner profile. Based on this, it recommends personalized course content that matches the student’s level, ensuring steady and confident progress.



Addressing the challenges faced by students with semantic dyscalculia.

This objective aims to build a personalized AR learning platform for semantic dyscalculia that helps students visualize math concepts interactively. Key features include emotion and concentration detection through facial expression analysis, and voice emotion recognition. The platform adapts learning activities in real time based on the student’s emotional state and engagement to create a supportive and effective learning experience.

Methodology

Unveiling the Evidence: Exploring Our Research Methodology for Enhancing Children's Practical Thinking

The methodology involves developing an interactive mobile system called "Giggle," which uses multisensory technologies to assess and support students with dyscalculia. It begins with an adaptive question math aptitude test tailored to Sri Lankan Grade 4 and 5 content, collecting both quantitative data (accuracy, response time) and qualitative insights from parents to build personalized learning paths. Three core models Question Mapping, Performance Analysis, and Personalized Course Generation work together to continuously monitor and adapt the learning content based on student performance and emotional state.


For interventions, the system targets the three main dyscalculia types using specialized modules.


  • (1) Semantic dyscalculia is addressed with AR-based lessons combined with real-time emotion and attention tracking using facial expression, voice, and gaze detection models.


  • (2) Procedural dyscalculia is supported through gesture-based learning and handwriting analysis using CNNs and linear regression to monitor accuracy and stepwise problem-solving.


  • (3) Verbal dyscalculia interventions include voice-driven instruction and practice using speech-to-text and emotion detection models,enabling interactive verbal math exercises and real-time feedback to improve verbal math skills.
